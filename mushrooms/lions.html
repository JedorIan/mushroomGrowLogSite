Lion's Mane

Hericium erinaceus

Appearance

Lion’s Mane grows in white, shaggy clusters with soft cascading spines. Its rounded shape and unique appearance make it instantly recognizable.

Texture and Flavor

Lion’s Mane has a dense interior that pulls apart easily, developing a tender, seafood-like texture when cooked. The flavor is mild, slightly sweet, and absorbs seasonings well.

Culinary Uses

Excellent sautéed, roasted, shredded, or seared. Works well in pasta, stir-fries, and as a seafood-style replacement in many dishes.

Additional Info

To best store dried mushrooms, store in a cool, dry place. To rehydrate, soak in warm water for 15–20 minutes, then squeeze gently and cook like fresh.
